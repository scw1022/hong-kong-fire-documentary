      3 arrested after highrise fire in Hong Kong kills dozens

      UpdatedNovember 26 · 6:52 PM EST
      Suspects arrested on suspicion of manslaughter, 279 people still missing

      Escaping from the deadly Hong Kong apartment fire

      November 27|
      Duration 1:02
      Dozens are dead and more still missing after a fire ripped through a Hong Kong highrise complex. Three men have been arrested and charged with manslaughter in connection with the fire.

      The Latest

      • At least 44 people are dead as of Thursday after a massive fire ripped through a highrise apartment complex in Hong Kong, with another 45 still in critical condition, police said.
      • Police have arrested three men, aged between 52 and 68, on suspicion of manslaughter in connection with the region's worst fire in three decades.
      • Authorities said the unusual installation of styrofoam material found in the buildings could have caused the blazes to spread faster.
      • By Thursday morning local time, at least 279 people were still missing.
      • Fire officials said high temperatures have made rescue operations difficult and that they were struggling to reach the upper floors.
      • The Wang Fuk Court housing complex is home to roughly 4,800 people in the Tai Po district in Hong Kong’s New Territories.

      • November 26 · 6:47 PM ESTPinki Wong

        All eight buildings in Wang Fuk Court were under construction, with mesh and bamboo scaffolding seen on their exteriors.

        Paul Chow has family who live in that estate, and is waiting for news from them. A former councillor for Hong Kong’s Tai Po District, he now lives in Toronto.

        He says his parents and sister were not home when the fire broke out, and are not allowed in to check the condition of the unit. Bamboo scaffolding is common in wall renovation, he says. But constructions like this are usually done part by part, in his political experience, to save money and impact fewer residents at a time.

        "It's also winter in Hong Kong, so the wind is very strong; it brings the fire everywhere,” he said. “When there was just a small-scale fire, it quickly became a disaster."

      • November 26 · 6:24 PM EST

        Suspects work for construction company: police

        Kevin Maimann

        Police say the three people arrested around 2 a.m. include two directors and an engineering consultant from a construction company, according to the South China Morning Post. The suspects are said to be between the ages of 52 and 68.

      • November 26 · 6:20 PM EST

        Styrofoam could have made fire spread more quickly: authorities

        Kevin Maimann

        Authorities said the fire’s rapid spread was “unusual” and that styrofoam material was found in the buildings, which could have helped the blazes grow faster, according to the South China Morning Post.

        Senior Superintendent Eileen Chung Lai-yee said the foam materials were installed by a construction engineering company, the Post reported.

        “Police have reason to believe that the company’s responsible persons were grossly negligent,” she said, “which led to the incident and caused the fire to spread rapidly, resulting in serious casualties.”

        We’ve mentioned that concerns have also been raised about bamboo scaffolding around the buildings. There have been at least three fires involving the material this year, according to the Association for the Rights of Industrial Accident Victims in Hong Kong.

      • November 26 · 5:55 PM EST

        200 fire vehicles, 100 ambulances on scene

        Kevin Maimann

        Hong Kong’s Fire Services Department has dispatched more than 200 fire vehicles and about 100 ambulances. More than 1,200 fire and ambulance personnel have been mobilized, according to a government press release.

        Officials say 11 jets and eight turntable ladders have been deployed to the apartment complex for water spraying, while 26 search-and-rescue teams are handling assistance calls at the scene.

        Police have also brought over more than 1,000 personnel to co-ordinate evacuation, rescue operations and emergency support.

        Death toll reaches 40

        Forty people are now confirmed dead and another 45 are in critical condition, Hong Kong officials told a news conference.

        A fire official said they expect to keep fighting the apartment blaze “at least” until the evening.

      • November 26 · 4:42 PM EST

        Firefighters struggle to reach top floors

        Kevin Maimann

        Firefighters struggled to reach the upper floors of the housing complex due to intense heat as they worked through the night.

        "The priority is to extinguish the fire and rescue the residents who are trapped," John Lee told reporters. "The second is to support the injured. The third is to support and recover. Then, we'll launch a thorough investigation."

        Hong Kong resident 'devastated' while watching home burn in deadly highrise fire

        Hong Kong resident Jason Kong looked on as flames ripped through the highrise complex where he lives on Wednesday. He said some of his neighbours were still unaccounted for and also feared for the fate of his 10-year-old poodle, Ber Ber, who he said remains in his apartment after police barred him from returning.

        A 65-year-old man who lives in Block One of the apartment complex told Reuters he had to leave his dog behind when he fled the fire.

        Jason Kong said he tried to go back inside to get his 10-year-old poodle, Ber Ber, at around 5 p.m. local time because the fire hadn’t yet spread to his building, but police wouldn't let him.

        "I do not know how my dog is doing upstairs now. I am afraid the smoke will suffocate him," he said.

        "But now as I watch, the fire is not burning so strong at my apartment."

        Kong said he is devastated for his friends and neighbours, especially those who haven't been able to find their loved ones.

        "I know some are still inside the building. Why is there no one saving them? Are we waiting for them … how should we deal with this?"
